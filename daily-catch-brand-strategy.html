00 — Overview

The Opportunity

The Daily Catch enters a crowded but largely newsletter-dominated space where the primary competitors live in email inboxes, not app stores. That is the key strategic opening: the app format is itself a differentiator. Users who have deleted their news apps because they create anxiety are not yet subscribed to a replacement. The Daily Catch can own that void.

The product's core promise — 5 stories, ~30 seconds each, no algorithms, no guilt — is genuinely distinctive and maps to a real cultural moment: widespread news fatigue combined with a desire to remain informed. The brand challenge is making that promise feel trustworthy, warm, and worth $0 of attention every morning.

Stay informed, not overwhelmed. In a world of infinite scroll, The Daily Catch is a deliberate choice — five stories, then you're done.

04 — Brand Strategy

Six Growth Pillars

1

Own "2 Minutes"

Make the 2-minute read a proprietary brand asset. Every piece of content — social, ads, PR — quantifies the time. "Your morning news in 2 minutes" should be as recognizable as a tagline can be.

2

The "Why It Matters" Hook

Competitors report what happened. You explain why it affects your life. This is the emotional differentiation. Every social post should demonstrate this with a specific example.

3

Anti-Doomscroll Positioning

News fatigue is a universal feeling in 2026. Actively contrast with the anxiety of traditional news apps. "No algorithm trying to keep you scrolling" is a powerful differentiator — say it often.

4

Morning Ritual Identity

Compete with coffee, not with CNN. The Daily Catch is a habit, not a news source. Build content that ritualizes it: "what I do before my first meeting," "my 2-minute morning routine."

5

Creator Partnerships

Partner with productivity, wellness, and lifestyle creators — not media personalities. Your audience follows people who help them live better. Infiltrate that space: "how I stay informed in 2 minutes."

6

Community & FOMO Loop

Build a social layer: "Today's 5 stories — which one surprised you?" Engagement creates FOMO in non-users. Sharing stories drives organic installs more than any paid campaign.

05 — Content Playbook

What to Actually Post

Each platform gets a distinct content engine — not the same post resized. Platform-native content is the difference between 200% follower growth and getting ignored.

🎵
TikTok

"60-Second Explainer" Series

Daily hook video: "Here's the one story everyone is talking about and why it actually matters for you." Raw-feeling, direct-to-camera or text-on-screen. Open with the payoff, not the setup. Use the word "you" constantly. Post daily at 7–9am. Treat captions as SEO keywords for Google indexing.

Daily posting Direct-to-camera Hook in 3 seconds SEO captions
📸
Instagram

Carousel "Story Cards" + Reels

Carousels: slide 1 = what happened, slide 2 = why it matters, slide 3 = how it affects you. Clean, typographic design with The Daily Catch aesthetic. Reels = repurposed TikTok. Stories daily: "Today's 5 stories — open the app." Use Stories to drive installs with a single tap link.

Carousel daily Story-driven Reels Stories → app install CTA
🧵
Threads

Story Thread Breakdowns

Take one of the day's 5 stories and break it into a 4-tweet thread: Post 1 = what happened (1 sentence). Post 2 = context. Post 3 = why it matters to you. Post 4 = "We covered this + 4 more in The Daily Catch. Link in bio." Low effort, high alignment with the app's format.

Text-native 4-post thread Daily or every-other-day
🎬
YouTube

Shorts Repurpose + Weekly "5 Stories" Roundup

Immediately repurpose TikTok as Shorts for additional indexed visibility. Once you have bandwidth: a weekly 5-minute "The Week's Most Important Stories" long-form video. This builds trust, search presence, and gives you a different content dimension from your daily format.

Shorts: daily repurpose Long-form: weekly SEO-optimized titles
𝕏
X / Twitter

PR & Journalist Outreach Only

Don't invest organic content effort here. Use X primarily for: engaging with journalists who cover your stories, announcing press coverage, and monitoring news trends for editorial decisions. Brand growth on X is flat. Your energy is better spent on TikTok and Instagram.

Reactive only Press mentions No original content
06 — Launch Roadmap

First 90 Days

Month 1
Weeks 1–4

Foundation & Identity

  • Lock brand voice guide and visual system (colors, fonts, templates)
  • Create TikTok and Instagram accounts — optimize bios with keywords
  • Build 10–15 posts in advance before going live (content buffer)
  • Identify 5–10 micro-creators in productivity/wellness/lifestyle niches to approach
  • Set up Threads account; begin daily story threads
  • Launch daily TikTok posting at 7–9am local time
Month 2
Weeks 5–8

Audience Building & First Partnerships

  • Activate first 2–3 creator partnerships (gifted placements, no cash needed at this stage)
  • Begin YouTube Shorts repurposing from TikTok (5 minutes additional effort per day)
  • A/B test two TikTok formats: talking head vs. text-on-screen
  • Launch a recurring series: "The Story Behind the Story" — weekly deeper dive
  • Send 3 press pitches to tech/app newsletters and productivity media
  • Engage actively in comments — answer questions, pin best comments
Month 3
Weeks 9–12

Optimize & Scale What Works

  • Analyze top-performing content: double down on winning format
  • Run first paid Meta/TikTok campaign using best-performing organic creative
  • Launch community engagement moment: "Which of today's 5 stories hit different?"
  • Approach second-tier creator partnerships with proof-of-concept metrics
  • Begin weekly YouTube long-form if bandwidth allows
  • Target first press feature in a mainstream outlet via journalist relationships on X
07 — Success Metrics

How to Measure What Matters

Track platform metrics weekly but evaluate strategy monthly. Vanity metrics (follower count) matter less than conversion metrics (installs, DAU, D7 retention). Focus on the funnel.
